# FDA 510(k) Analysis: Home IPL Devices

---

## Key Finding: No Home IPL Device Has Skin Rejuvenation Clearance

Every single FDA-cleared home IPL device is cleared under **product code OHT**, which is exclusively for **"removal of unwanted body hair" only**. There are zero OHT-cleared devices with photorejuvenation, skin tone, hyperpigmentation, or redness reduction claims. This is not a gap in the research — it is confirmed by searching the FDA 510(k) database.

---

## FDA Product Code Landscape for Light Devices

| Code | Device Type | Indication | Reg. Class |
|------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **OHT** | OTC IPL hair removal | Unwanted body hair only | Class II |
| **OHS** | OTC LED light wrinkle reduction | Wrinkle reduction (non-ablative) | Class II |
| **OLP** | OTC acne light devices | Acne treatment | Class II |
| GEX | Professional IPL/laser (clinic) | Multiple including photorejuvenation | Class II/III |

**For your skin goals**: The OHS classification (LED wrinkle reduction) or an OTC device with dermal claims would require separate clearance through a different pathway — and no IPL device has achieved this for home use as of 2025.

### Why "Special 510(k)" Matters
The Special 510(k) pathway is faster than standard 510(k) because the manufacturer already holds the predicate clearance. Fansizhe used this to upgrade specs on existing cleared models and add new models. The FDA accepted their demonstration that the higher-energy specs don't create new safety risks vs the K251173 predicate.

## Critical Observations

### 1. The "27.5J" T055 Advertised on Alibaba
The FDA-cleared spec for T055 is **25J** (K253881). The Alibaba listing claiming 27.5J is almost certainly advertising the summed total of three sub-pulses. When the device fires a triple pulse train, Fansizhe's marketing adds up P1+P2+P3 and calls it "27.5J total output." The actual peak single-pulse fluence is much lower — approximately 8.33/3 = ~2.8 J/cm² per sub-pulse if evenly distributed. This is why the user correctly identified this as inadequate.

### 2. The 510nm Models Are NOT in Any FDA Clearance
None of Fansizhe's 510nm lower-cutoff models (T023A, T021A, T021K, T015K, T015C, T026, T035K, T016K, T058, T036K, T012, T001 series) appear in K251173 or K253881. These models carry NMPA (China SFDA), CE, and sometimes FCC clearance — but **not FDA OHT clearance**. This is likely because:
1. The 510nm models were not submitted for FDA review, OR
2. The FDA review was pending (T038 was noted as "FDA PENDING" in the catalog)

**Practical implication:** For the US market, the 510nm Fansizhe models are not FDA cleared. However, the user is buying from Alibaba for personal use, not reselling. Regulatory status affects import legality, not personal purchase risk. The device safety is certified by NMPA and CE, which are credible.

### 3. FDA Clearance ≠ Clinical Efficacy for Your Goals
FDA OHT clearance means:
- ✅ Device is safe for hair removal
- ✅ Device actually produces its rated energy output
- ❌ Does NOT mean it works for redness, pigmentation, or photorejuvenation
- ❌ Does NOT mean it has any FDA-cleared skin rejuvenation claim

The clearance is a safety and performance confirmation, not an efficacy endorsement for the user's goals.
