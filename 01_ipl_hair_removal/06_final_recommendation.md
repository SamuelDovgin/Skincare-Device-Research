### ⚠️ The metric that actually matters for sun spots: SINGLE-PULSE fluence

**The tables above are accumulated multi-pulse totals — the WRONG number for pigment work.** For discrete sun spots (solar lentigines), the correct treatment mode is a **single pulse**: it delivers energy into the melanin faster than the lesion's thermal relaxation time, so the spot darkens, micro-crusts, and sheds over 1–2 weeks (selective photothermolysis). Multi-pulse / SHR mode does the opposite by design — it spreads energy across 2–3 sub-pulses with cooling gaps to keep the epidermis cool while deeper hair follicles slowly heat. That is ideal for hair and counterproductive for pigment. **So for sun spots, peak single-pulse fluence is the only spec that counts, and every triple-pulse "total" above is irrelevant.**

**One device now clears it with measured proof: the T023A IGBT at 6.08 J/cm² single flash** (seller video, 2026-06-14; 18.23J ÷ 3cm²). That is the first device in either catalog with a *verified* ≥6 J/cm² single pulse — and it is 510nm, the right wavelength. The next-best verified numbers are ~5.75 (T011C) and ~5.5 (the FDA-cleared T023A baseline). Beyond the T023A, the only *theoretical* path higher is a **small-aperture head** that concentrates fixed energy into less area — but those remain unverified:

### The one question that resolves the entire search

Ask every seller, verbatim:

> **"What is the maximum fluence in SINGLE-PULSE mode — not the double/triple/SHR total? And for the small head specifically?"**

- If they answer ≥6 J/cm² single-pulse for the T012 IGBT or T012C small head **and** send a test report → that's your device.
- Otherwise the realistic best is the **T023A at 6.08 J/cm² single-pulse, 510nm** — which the 2026-06-14 video already confirms *meets* your 6 J/cm² target and is the strongest verified single-pulse option on the right wavelength. The T012 small-head numbers would only matter if you want to push materially past 6.

### Safety note specific to this plan
Single-pulse pigment targeting is also where IPL's PIH risk is highest (doc 07). For discrete sun spots on lighter (Fitzpatrick I–III) skin it is a legitimate, effective technique; on pigment-prone skin the same single pulse that clears a lentigo can trigger post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. Patch test, start low, and re-read doc 07 before treating the face.
