# FDA 510(k) Analysis: Home IPL Devices

---

## Key Finding: No Home IPL Device Has Skin Rejuvenation Clearance

Every FDA-cleared **home/OTC** IPL device — including all Fansizhe and Ulike models — is cleared under **product code OHT**, whose authorized indication is **"removal of unwanted body hair" only**. None of these clearances authorizes any photorejuvenation, skin-tone, hyperpigmentation, or redness claim. Pigment and vascular indications ("benign pigmented lesions," "diffuse redness") do exist in the FDA system, but only for **professional/clinic** IPL systems (e.g., Candela Nordlys, product code GEX) — not for any consumer home device verified in this research.

> **Verified directly from the filings.** The "Indications for Use" pages for K223928, K251173, K253881, and K260518 all limit the home IPL devices to unwanted hair removal. Nothing in these filings authorizes pigmentation, redness, or photorejuvenation claims.

*(Note: an earlier draft cited "162 OHT devices, all hair removal." The exact count was not independently confirmed in this pass and has been removed; the substantive point — that OHT = hair removal only, with zero home rejuvenation clearances found — is confirmed.)*

## FDA Product Code Landscape for Light Devices

| Code | Device Type | Indication | Reg. Class |
|------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **OHT** | OTC IPL hair removal | Unwanted body hair only | Class II |
| **OHS** | OTC LED light wrinkle reduction | Wrinkle reduction (non-ablative) | Class II |
| **OLP** | OTC acne light devices | Acne treatment | Class II |
| GEX | Professional IPL/laser (clinic) | Multiple including photorejuvenation | Class II/III |

**For your skin goals**: The OHS classification (LED wrinkle reduction) or an OTC device with dermal claims would require separate clearance through a different pathway — and no IPL device found in this research has achieved this for home use as of June 2026.

## Critical Observations

### 1. The "27.5J" T055 Advertised on Alibaba
The FDA-cleared spec for T055 is **25J** (K253881). The Alibaba listing claiming 27.5J is almost certainly advertising the summed total of three sub-pulses. When the device fires a triple pulse train, Fansizhe's marketing adds up P1+P2+P3 and calls it "27.5J total output." The actual peak single-pulse fluence is much lower — approximately 8.33/3 = ~2.8 J/cm² per sub-pulse if evenly distributed. This is why the user correctly identified this as inadequate.

### 2. CORRECTION — Several 510nm Fansizhe Models ARE FDA Cleared
An earlier draft of this document stated that *none* of Fansizhe's 510nm models are FDA cleared. **That was wrong.** At minimum:
- **K221569** clears T013C/T015C/T015K at 510–1200nm, 16.1J / 4.03 J/cm²
- **K223928** clears T023K/T023A/T023B/T023C/T023D/T023E, T021K/T021A, T001A/T001B/T001M/T001N, T011C, and T016K at 510–1200nm

This also resolves a contradiction in the Fansizhe catalog, which marked T013C as "No FDA." The catalog appears to be outdated or inconsistent; the FDA database is authoritative — T013C is cleared. (Doc 04's catalog transcription has been annotated accordingly.)

**What remains true:** The *newer high-fluence catalog claims* are not all FDA-verified. T023A is FDA-cleared by name under K223928, but only at 16.6J / 5.5 J/cm² in that filing, not the catalog's 25J / 8.33 J/cm². T012 IGBT, T058, and T036K still need exact K-number verification if the seller claims FDA clearance for those higher-output versions.

**Practical implication:** For personal purchase from Alibaba, FDA status affects US import/resale legality, not personal-use risk. NMPA + CE are credible safety certifications. But "FDA cleared" claims on individual Alibaba listings should be matched to a specific K-number — ask the seller which clearance covers the exact model, and verify it at the FDA 510(k) database.

### 3. FDA Clearance ≠ Clinical Efficacy for Your Goals
FDA OHT clearance means:
- ✅ Device is safe for hair removal
- ✅ Device actually produces its rated energy output
- ❌ Does NOT mean it works for redness, pigmentation, or photorejuvenation
- ❌ Does NOT mean it has any FDA-cleared skin rejuvenation claim

The clearance is a safety and performance confirmation, not an efficacy endorsement for the user's goals.
