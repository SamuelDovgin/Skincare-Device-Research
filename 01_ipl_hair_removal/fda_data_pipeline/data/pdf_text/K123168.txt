General Device Description:
The 0-Plus T + IPL Laser System is intended for the incision, excision, ablation and vaporization
of soft tissue for general dermatology. The device includes a 0-Switched Nd:YAG laser source
with a max energy of 1000mJ at 1064nm and a max energy of 500mJ at 532nm, a 0-Switched
Ruby laser source with a max energy of lOOOmJ at 694nm, and an intense pulse light (IPL) hand
piece. The 0-Switched lasers are intended for treatment of pigmented lesions and tattoo
removal. The optical delivery system for the three laser wavelengths is an articulated arm with
replaceable hand pieces with 2, 3, 4, and 6 mm spot sizes. The IPL hand piece, depending on
the particular wavelength filter used, is intended for hair removal, treatment of dermatological
vascular lesions, benign pigmented lesions and inflammatory acne (acne vulgaris). The IPL hand
pieces are provided in a fixed or a replaceable configuration.

Comparison of Technological Characteristics:
The a-Plus T + IFPL Laser System has substantially equivalent technological characteristics to the
predicate devices. The ten subsystems in the 0-Plus T + IPL laser system contain all nine
subsystems of the 0-Plus T (K073549) with the addition of the IPL hand piece subsystem that is
cleared with the Ultrawave Ill EX 1320 (K083207).
The a-Switched Nd:YAG laser source with a max energy of lOOOmJ at 1064nm and a max
energy of 500mJ at 532nm is identical to the 0-Switched Nd:YAG laser source utilized in the 0Plus T device.. Also, the a-Switched Ruby laser source with a max energy of lOOOmJ at 694nm
is identical the 0-Switched Ruby laser source utilized in the 0-Plus T device. The optical delivery
system for the three laser wavelengths is an articulated arm that is also used in the a-Plus T
device. The IPL hand piece portion of the a-Plus T + IPL Laser System is the same as the IPL
hand piece portion of the Ultrawave Ill EX 1320. In addition, the 0-Plus T + IPL laser system
includes a power supply, a cooling system, an optical delivery system, a microprocessor based

Comparison of Intended Use:
The a-Plus T + IPL laser system is a laser surgical instrument for use in general surgery and
dermatology intended for the incision, excision, ablation, and vaporization of soft tissue and for
the treatment of vascular and pigmented lesions, tattoo removal, hair removal, and inflammatory
acne (acne vulgaris).
The intended use of the laser portion of the 0-Plus T + IPL laser system is the same as the
intended use of the previously cleared 0-Plus T as no changes have been made with regard to
performance or technology to the a-Plus T. Furthermore, no changes have been made to the
IPL hand piece portion of the subject device since its initial clearance in K051113 and its
clearance for use with the Ultrawave Ill EX 1320 in K083207. Therefore, the intended use of the
IPL hand piece used with the 0-Pius T + IPL laser system is substantially equivalent to the
intended use of the IPL hand piece used with the Ultrawave Ill EX 1320.
Substantial Equivalence:
The Quanta System a-Plus T + IPL laser system is as safe and effective as the predicate
devices. The 0-Plus T + IPL laser system has the same intended use and similar technological
characteristics and principles of operation as its predicate devices. The minor technological
differences between the 0-Plus T + IPL laser system and its predicate devices raise no new
issues of substantial equivalence or safety and effectiveness. Thus, the 0-Plus T + IPL laser
system is substantially equivalent to its identified predicate devices.
