### 2a. What exactly is Focuslight? (you asked — not a splash page) ✅

**Focuslight Technologies Inc.** is a **large, publicly-traded photonics company** — not a marketing page, and not a beauty brand.
- Founded **2007** (Dr. Victor X. Liu), HQ **Xi'an, China**; **publicly listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange STAR Market (688167)** since its Dec-2021 IPO.
- **900+ employees** across China, Germany, Switzerland, Singapore, Malaysia; **400+ patents**; ISO 9001/14001/45001 + IATF 16949. Grew via acquisitions of **LIMO GmbH (2017)** and **SUSS MicroOptics (2024)**.
- Core business: **high-power diode-laser components, modules, and micro-optics** (photon generation + photon control) for optical comms, semiconductor, advanced manufacturing, automotive, medical, etc.

**Their relationship to beauty devices — they're a *component supplier to manufacturers*, B2B only:**
- The skin-rejuvenation page offers a **"home-use laser skin rejuvenation module"**: **1470nm non-ablative fractional**, eye-safe, customizable **single-spot / multi-spot / dot-matrix** output (≈1.5 W, 6–12 mJ/spot), plus a beam-homogenizer/flat-top optic.
- **No pricing, no MOQ, no "buy now," no consumer sales.** The page's only CTA is *"contact our sales teams."*

**So, directly answering you:**
- *Splash page?* **No** — real public company.
- *Do they supply beauty-laser companies?* **Yes** — they sell the **laser engine/module** that a device maker (e.g. a YDUNVIE-type brand) would build a finished handheld around.
- *Can you buy direct?* **Not a finished device.** You'd engage their **sales/OEM** team to source **modules** and commission a build — realistic only if you're manufacturing a product, not buying one unit. For a single consumer device, go to **YDUNVIE/MimiSilk** instead; Focuslight matters only if you want to **build your own**.

## 5. Screening every supplier (send after first contact)

1. Are you the **manufacturer**, a trading company, or a distributor?
2. Exact **legal manufacturer name** on the FDA/CE documents?
3. **510(k) number** — and does it cover *this exact model* or only a similar one?
4. Provide the **device label, IFU/manual, and UDI**.
5. Is it **OTC/home-use** or **prescription/professional-use**?
6. Exact output **per level**: wavelength (±nm), J/cm² **and/or** mJ/pulse, pulse width, spot size, fractional density.
7. Is the J/cm² figure **single-pulse, pulse-train total, or full-treatment dose**? *(Same trap as the parent IPL repo — multi-pulse "totals" inflate headline numbers.)*
8. Which **lab** measured the optical output? Provide the report.
9. **Authorization letter** from the 510(k) holder for private label/export.

## 6. Red flags
- "FDA approved" / "FDA registered" with **no K-number** (registration ≠ clearance)
- "Medical CE" with no notified-body/MDR detail
- States "1450nm laser" but **can't give J/cm² or mJ/pulse**
- "High energy" with **no pulse width, spot size, or beam profile**
- **No contact sensor / no eye-safety (IEC 60825-1) report**
- Home-use claims for **melasma, mole/tattoo removal, or ablative resurfacing**
- A "fractional" listing that is actually **CO₂, IPL, RF, or 808nm hair removal** in disguise
