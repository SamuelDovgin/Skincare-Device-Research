## 2. The home **fractional** devices (the real comparables)

### Tria FRX — the only buyable, FDA-cleared, verifiable home fractional laser ★★★★
- ✅ **FDA:** Tria FAN System (K130459) + FANp (K141868). Semiconductor diode, **1450 ± 50 nm**, fractional non-ablative, cleared for **periorbital wrinkles**. Predicates include Palomar LOI, Cynosure Iluminage, Affirm, Solta Fraxel re:fine.
- ⚠️ Marketing: ~5–12 mJ/pulse; ~$650–$700 retail; "12 weeks on + 4 weeks off" cadence; glide handpiece; moderate-to-high pain, some redness/peeling.
- **Verdict:** the strongest *trustworthy* home option for the **collagen/texture** goal. Conservative on pigment (its own labeling cautions dyschromia/freckles may not respond). The one to buy if you want FDA paperwork + a real company behind it.

### PaloVia Skin Renewing Laser (Palomar LOI) — closest historical match ★★★★
- ✅ **FDA:** Palomar LOI System K090525, OTC, periorbital wrinkles. **1410 ± 5 nm**, handheld fractional with an **internal scanner** stepping a microbeam through an array. Pivotal trial: ~90% had some periocular wrinkle improvement.
- **Status:** discontinued. This is the *root predicate* for much of the category (NIRA and Tria both cite Palomar lineage).
- **Verdict:** historically the purest "home Fraxel." Study target, not a buy.

### Philips RéAura SC5000 — the "Fraxel-at-home" ★★★★
- ✅ **1435nm diode, fractionated non-ablative, co-developed with Solta (Fraxel maker).** Marketed as creating thousands of microscopic beams to renew skin and push out pigmented cells.
- **Status:** discontinued (EU/UK). Possibly the most explicitly Fraxel-derived consumer device ever sold.
- **Verdict:** the other big "forgotten" device worth studying alongside PaloVia.

### YDUNVIE Iris Ice / Iris Ice Plus — strongest *current* China-market fractional ★★★½
- ⚠️ Seller specs: **1450nm**, "nano-matrix" fractional, contact/"ice" cooling, 3–12 mJ/pulse (Plus), 180 J full-face, DynamicScan, depth modes ~300/350/400 µm, ~10 min full-face, 3 treatments/charge.
- **Iris Ice Plus vs Iris Ice:** *probably not meaningfully stronger* — both top out around **12 mJ/pulse**. "Plus" appears to add cooling, sensors, app/zones, shell/UX, and certification marketing rather than raw output. Don't pay a big premium without a same-units spec comparison.
- ✅ Likely manufacturer: **Jiangsu Unimed Laser** (FDA K182498). See [04_suppliers_and_companies](04_suppliers_and_companies.md).
- **Verdict:** the most credible China fractional option for **texture/pores/acne marks/collagen** — *if* specs and authorization are verified. Buy through an authorized agent, not a dropship store.

### MimiSilk Iris — likely a YDUNVIE-family relabel ★★★½
- ⚠️ Sells "1450nm advanced at-home fractional laser," ~$599, ~100 µm microbeam, ≤12 mJ, ~2.11 J/cm² max density. Reddit + reseller trails (myernk = "authorized YDUNVIE agent") suggest MimiSilk is the **same Jiangsu Unimed/YDUNVIE hardware** under a Western brand.
- ✅/⚠️ **FDA clearance now near-confirmed via name match:** MimiSilk's product is titled *"1450nm **Advanced Skin Renewing Device**,"* which is the **verbatim device name of Jiangsu Unimed's 510(k) K182498** (cleared Nov 30 2018, product code ONG, microbeam/fractional). So MimiSilk *is* FDA-cleared — but on the **manufacturer's** filing, not a brand-held one (still worth confirming the exact UDI/label on the unit you'd buy).
- **Verdict:** plausibly identical hardware to Iris Ice with English-market support; verify the regulatory claim and the factory link before trusting.

### YDUNVIE Dora / Dora Plus / Dora Pro — the home **1927nm pigment** option ★★★½
- ⚠️ Seller specs: **1927nm** non-ablative fractional, 2–7 mJ/pulse, dark spots/renewal/brightening. A related "A8 / 大白" listing describes 1927nm fractional for epidermal renewal + melanin metabolism, 3 gears, 2–3×/week, 20–25 min/session (page copy is internally inconsistent — verify).
- **Verdict:** the most on-target *wavelength* for **your pigment goal** in the home market — a home analog of Perméa/Moxi/LaseMD. But 1927nm pigment devices can also *worsen* PIH if poorly dosed; verify energy/density/cooling and ramp carefully.

### Focuslight 1470nm home module — OEM *component*, not a product ★★★
- ✅ Focuslight (Xi'an; **public, SSE STAR 688167**, 900+ staff, 400+ patents) supplies a **1470nm** home skin-rejuvenation **module**: 1.5 W, 100×100 µm spot, **6–12 mJ/spot**, single/multi/dot-matrix output; they explicitly benchmark RéAura and Tria and note home NAF spot energy is generally ≤~15 mJ/spot.
- **Verdict:** a **B2B laser-engine supplier**, not a consumer brand — they sell the module a device maker builds around. Relevant only if you want to **commission/OEM your own device**; for a single unit, buy YDUNVIE/MimiSilk instead. Full company profile in [04 §2a](04_suppliers_and_companies.md).

## 3. The adjacent (gentler) categories — often mis-marketed as equivalent

### NIRA (Model 2 / Precision / Pro) — non-fractional warmer ★★
- ✅ **FDA:** K163137 (Model 1), K222685 (Model 2). **1450 ± 20 nm, non-fractional, non-ablative.** Model 2: 2 W, spot 12.5×13.8 mm², fluence **2.1–3.8 J/cm²**, fan cooling. Owner: **Dermal Photonics Corp** (US). ✅ 76-subject blinded study showed periorbital wrinkle improvement.
- **Verdict:** legit, low-downtime **collagen maintenance** (esp. under-eye). **Not** a resurfacer, **weak for pigment**. Don't expect Clear + Brilliant results.

### Iluminage Skin Smoothing Laser — same wavelength; fractional status disputed ★★–★★★
- ✅ **FDA:** K133473 (Cynosure). **1440nm, 3–4 J/cm², 7mm spot**, periorbital/perioral wrinkles. FDA description = energy over a fixed area, **not clearly fractional**.
- ⚠️ **Retail listing** ([myernk, ~$598, reg $833](https://myernk.com/fr/products/iluminage-at-home-skin-smoothing-anti-aging-laser-fda-cleared)): describes it as a **non-ablative *fractional* laser**, **1440nm band**, **380 mJ (low) / 500 mJ (high)**, **"36 light spots per second"**, **12 min/session every 3 days**, no cooling mentioned. Claims: fine lines, pores, acne scars, uneven tone, spots; clinical 78%/87%/95% fine-line improvement at 2/4/12 weeks. Ships from US warehouse; **cannot ship outside the USA**. Seller = myernk (HK), the same reseller that lists YDUNVIE Iris Ice.
- **The discrepancy to resolve:** the FDA record (non-fractional, 3–4 J/cm² over a 7mm spot) and the retail listing (fractional, mJ-per-spot, 36 spots/s) **don't match**. Either the listing is marketing spin on the bulk K133473 device, or it's a newer/different (possibly relabeled) fractional unit sold under the Iluminage name. **Unit check:** if "380–500 mJ" is the *total across 36 spots*, that's ~10.5–13.9 mJ/microbeam — squarely in the Tria/Clear+Brilliant fractional range; if it's per-spot, it's implausibly high. Force the seller to clarify (see [06 RFQ](06_sourcing_rfq_templates.md)).
- **Verdict:** same wavelength family as C+B Original. **If genuinely fractional at ~10–14 mJ/microbeam, it jumps to ★★★** and becomes a real Tria FRX alternative (notably *without* cooling — a downside for pigment-prone skin). If it's the bulk K133473 device, it stays ★★. Verify the fractional claim + which K-number it actually ships under before buying. **US-only shipping** is a hard constraint.

### LYMA Laser / PRO — LLLT/photobiomodulation, not resurfacing ★
- ✅ **FDA:** K210823 / K243330 (LYMA Life, UK). **808nm NIR + 620nm red, 62.5 mW/cm², 8 cm²**. Predicates are LED/LLLT devices.
- **Verdict:** photobiomodulation — gentle, no downtime, general skin quality. A *different category* from fractional resurfacing despite premium "laser" marketing.

### JOVS masks (Shenzhen Qianyu) — VCSEL PBM ★
- ✅ **FDA:** K244020 (JM1/JM2B) **660 + 850nm VCSEL**, 40–100 mW/cm². Also IPL hair-removal clearances (K214113, K220645).
- **Verdict:** wearable PBM/red-light, not resurfacing.

### DermRays Revive (Wuhan Lotuxs) — 1064nm, wrong category ★
- ✅ **FDA:** K231910. **1064nm diode, 400ms, 5–10 J/cm², 15mm spot, prescription use.** For wrinkles + hair removal.
- **Verdict:** different wavelength (targets via different mechanism), and **Rx not OTC** — not a Clear + Brilliant analog.

## 4. Professional / clinical relatives (context for "what efficacious looks like")

| Device | Wavelength | Role |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Clear + Brilliant Original / Perméa | 1440 / 1927nm | entry-level in-office NAF (the benchmark) |
| Sciton **Moxi** | 1927nm | clinic pigment/tone/prevention NAF |
| **LaseMD / LaseMD Ultra** | 1927nm thulium | clinic pigment + topical-delivery microchannels |
| **Fraxel re:store / Dual** | 1550 / 1927nm | the higher-power origin of the category |
| Cynosure **Affirm** | 1440 / 1320nm | clinic NAF wrinkle/pigment |

These define the upper bound. Home devices are deliberately a fraction of these — useful for calibrating expectations.

## 5. Final ranking for the user's goals

**For pigment / PIH / brightness (1927nm physics):**
1. *In-office:* Clear + Brilliant **Perméa** / Sciton Moxi / LaseMD
2. *At-home:* **YDUNVIE Dora (1927nm)** ⚠️ — verify specs/authorization
3. *Reality check:* topicals + SPF still beat any home laser on pigment (see parent [07](../01_ipl_hair_removal/07_alternatives_and_strategy.md))

**For collagen / texture / pores / fine lines (1450nm physics):**
1. *In-office:* Clear + Brilliant **Original** / Fraxel
2. *At-home, trustworthy:* **Tria FRX** ✅ (FDA-cleared, real company)
3. *At-home, China-market:* **YDUNVIE Iris Ice / Iris Ice Plus** ⚠️ (or MimiSilk Iris) — verify
4. *Gentle maintenance:* **NIRA** ✅ (non-fractional)

**The "forgotten" devices worth studying:** **PaloVia** and **Philips RéAura** — the two purest home-Fraxel devices ever sold (both discontinued).
