## 0. TL;DR

1. **It's the Tria Age-Defying Laser under a new name.** SmoothBeauty™ Laser = **Tria Age-Defying Laser** = **Tria FRX** = model **SRL** ("Skin Rejuvenating Laser"). Same hardware, rebranded over time/region; your bilingual EN/FR manual is the **Canada/international** edition. ✅ FDA-cleared **Oct 30, 2013** (the repo's **K130459**). It is **not** the *only* FDA-cleared home fractional laser — **MimiSilk Iris** (✅ cleared via Jiangsu Unimed's **K182498**, "Advanced Skin Renewing Device") is too, and **PaloVia/RéAura** were historically. What's true: **Tria is the only one whose own brand holds a verifiable 510(k) under its own name for a currently-sold device** — the "real company, real clearance" pick ([03](03_device_landscape.md)/[04](04_suppliers_and_companies.md)/[README](README.md)).
2. **Verified specs (from your IFU, p.31):** **1440 nm diode laser**, fractional non-ablative, **Class 1** (invisible beam, eye-safe by design), **5–12 mJ/pulse**, model **SRL**, 3-yr design life. ✅ — these were ⚠️ in the repo before; now they're primary-source confirmed.
3. **Energy ≈ in-office; density is ~10–30× lower.** Per-pulse 5–12 mJ matches Clear+Brilliant's 4–9 mJ, and depth (~300–450 µm) is wavelength-locked and equal. The gap is **coverage/density** — this device lays down only **~30–180 MTZ/cm² (~0.3–1.5% coverage) per session** 🔍 vs C+B's ~650–1,270/10%. Full math in [08 §3b](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md). A web anchor corroborates the floor: at-home units "usually do not exceed ~40 MTZ/cm²."
4. **It's a collagen/fine-line tool, NOT a pigment tool.** 1440 nm = water/collagen depth. The IFU's *cleared indication is periorbital wrinkles (crow's feet)*, and it **explicitly says it is not effective on dyschromia, freckles, or veins**. For *your* pigment goal it's off-target (you'd want 1927 nm — see [09](09_1927nm_vs_1450nm_headtohead.md)); for the *collagen/texture half* of your goal it's on-target.
5. **The protocol:** cleanse → treat 4 face zones nightly → serum + AM sunscreen. **Ramp Level 1 → 2 → 3** as skin adjusts. **5 nights/week for a 12-week course**, max once per 24 h. Jump to [§5 Quick Start](#5-quick-start--everything-you-need-to-know-to-treat-tonight).

## 3. The "coverage, density & energy" you asked for

The IFU publishes energy and timing but — like every home device — **not** coverage % or MTZ density. Those are reconstructed in [08 §3b](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md); here's the device-specific summary, now anchored to *your* IFU dwell times.

| Parameter | This device | How we know |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-pulse energy** | **5–12 mJ** | ✅ IFU |
| **Microbeam / MTZ diameter** | ~100–150 µm | 🔍 NAFL class ([08 §4b](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)) |
| **Depth** | **~300–450 µm** | 🔍 *wavelength-set* by 1440 nm — same as C+B Original; **not** a Tria advantage |
| **Per-column fluence** | ~64–150 J/cm² (5–12 mJ into ~100 µm) | 🔍 high, even ≥ C+B — intensity isn't the gap |
| **Per-session MTZ density** | **~30–180 MTZ/cm²** (L3/12 mJ ≈ low end ~38; L1/5 mJ ≈ higher count, lower energy) | 🔍 [08 §3b]; web anchor: home units "do not exceed ~40 MTZ/cm²" |
| **Per-session coverage** | **~0.3–1.5%** of skin surface | 🔍 [08 §3b]; Tria patent discloses a 0.25–5% *design* envelope ⚠️ |
| **vs Clear+Brilliant (high)** | C+B ≈ **650–1,270 MTZ/cm², 10%** → this device is **~10–30× lower density** | ✅/🔍 [08](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md) |

**How the levels move density:** the level controls **dwell time per zone** (30 → 60 → 150 s). More dwell = more glides = more pulses laid into the same skin = **more cumulative MTZs**. That's the IFU's own qualitative claim ("as you go up in levels… the laser will create more MTZs"), quantified: **Level 3 ≈ 5× the per-zone treatment time of Level 1.** So "increase your level" literally means "increase your density" — the lever that matters most ([08 §3](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)).

**Bottom line:** energy and depth are genuinely C+B-class; **what's small is density per session** — which is *by design* so daily unsupervised use stays safe. The way you recover ground is **frequency over months**, not power (see [11](11_subscriber_qa_lowdensity_rejuvenation.md) and [08 §3c](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)).

## 4. What it's actually *for* (read this before you set expectations)

There are **three different claim levels** floating around this device — keep them straight:

| Claim layer | What it says | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **IFU cleared indication** (the legal one) | **Periorbital wrinkles ("crow's feet")** only | ✅ narrow |
| **Treatment protocol** | Treat the **full face** in 4 zones | ✅ that's the labeled usage |
| **Marketing** | "Multiple signs of facial aging, entire face, all skin tones; 76% wrinkle improvement, 95% smoother" ⚠️ | ⚠️ vendor study |

And the IFU is blunt about what it will **not** do — it lists these as "do not use, may not be effective":

- ❌ **Dyschromia / brown spots** ("may not be effective… you may want to see your doctor")
- ❌ **Freckles**
- ❌ **Veins**
- ❌ **Pimples / active acne**

**For your goals** (pigment + collagen, per [README](README.md)):
- **Collagen / fine lines / texture** → ✅ on-target. This is exactly what a 1440 nm fractional does. Realistic, evidence-backed *modest* improvement with consistent use.
- **Pigment / brightening / PIH** → ❌ off-target. Wrong wavelength (you want **1927 nm**, [09](09_1927nm_vs_1450nm_headtohead.md)), and the IFU explicitly disclaims pigment. Worse: in pigment-prone skin, fractional **density drives PIH** — and **27% of trial users had temporary skin darkening (5 of 60 lasting >3 months)**. So if your skin is reactive, this device can *cause* the exact problem you're trying to fix. Use conservatively and watch for darkening (§7).

## 5. Quick Start — everything you need to know to treat tonight

### A. One-time setup
1. **Charge fully** — seat it in the cradle; the battery light **flashes while charging and turns off when full** (≤2.5 h). May arrive charged. Cradle works on any 100–240 V outlet (travel plug adapter only).
2. **Unlock** — if it won't power on, it's locked. **Hold the power button until you hear 4 beeps**, then release. (To lock later: hold power until the padlock icon lights.)
3. **Patch test (first-timers)** — run it on your **outer forearm** at the level you intend to use; **wait 24 h**. If no blistering/scabbing (rare), proceed to the face.

### B. Every-night routine (the 3-step loop)
1. **PREP** — wash and **dry** your face. Remove **all** makeup, sunscreen, and product (they block the laser). Tria's Priming Cleanser is the marketed match; any thorough cleanse works.
2. **TREAT** — see the zone method below.
3. **FINISH** — apply a soothing serum/moisturizer (Tria's Finishing Serum is retinol + peptides; any gentle serum works). **In the morning: broad-spectrum SPF, every day — non-negotiable.**

### C. How to treat — the 4-zone method
1. Lift it off the cradle, press **power**, confirm it's **unlocked**.
2. Press **power** to pick your level (it remembers your last). **Start at Training Level 1.**
3. **Divide the face into 4 zones** (per the IFU diagram): **Zone 3 = forehead**, **Zone 1 = one cheek/mid-face**, **Zone 2 = other cheek**, **Zone 4 = chin/lower face**. Crow's-feet area is treated as part of the adjacent zone.
4. Lay the **tip flat** on the skin and **glide slowly side-to-side**, covering the whole zone. The **contact light glows purple only when you're doing it right** (flat + moving + good contact) — and the laser only fires then. Forehead: you can also go up-and-down. **Keep moving — don't park on one spot.**
5. **Treat each zone until it beeps**, then move on: it beeps **once** after Zone 1, **twice** after Zone 2, **three times** after Zone 3, then a **completion tone + auto shut-off** after Zone 4. Don't advance before the beep (causes under-treatment of that zone).

### D. Hard "do nots" while treating
- ❌ **Never on the eye, eyelid, or inside the eye socket.** Around the eyes, treat **only where you can feel bone under the skin.**
- ❌ Never aim into your eyes (invisible beam; Class 1 but still).
- ❌ Not over a zone **more than once per 24 h** (raises irritation risk).
- ❌ Not on tattoos/permanent makeup, broken/infected/burned/sunburned skin.

## 6. The 12-week course & the level ramp

| Week(s) | Level | Per-zone | Full-face | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ~1 (adjust) | **Training L1** | 30 s | ~2 min | Let skin acclimate; confirm no bad reaction |
| ~2–3 | **Treatment L2** | 60 s | ~4 min | Step up energy + density as tolerated |
| ~4–12 | **Treatment L3** | 150 s | ~10 min | "Best results" level per IFU |

- **Frequency:** **5 nights/week** (evenings, so overnight redness fades). ✅ IFU.
- **Course length:** the IFU says **do not exceed a 12-week course.** ⚠️ Common practice is a break then a repeat/maintenance course, but that's beyond the labeled instruction — your call.
- **Why ramp:** higher level = more energy *and* more density (§3). Move up only when the current level feels comfortable; if L3 stings too much, stay at L2 — consistency beats intensity for this device class ([11](11_subscriber_qa_lowdensity_rejuvenation.md)).
- **Realistic timeline:** users report first changes ~2 weeks; "best results" several weeks after the 12-week course (collagen remodels over months). The honest size of the effect is **modest** — a real fraction of a Clear+Brilliant course, not equal to it ([08 §6](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)).

## 7. What to expect & when to STOP

**Normal, expected (from the 60-person trial, ✅ IFU):** redness (95%), stinging/prickling (92%), burning (83%), warmth (78%); also dry skin, mild swelling, flaking, and **temporary small brown dots** (the healing MTZ crusts — normal, flake off in days). Most resolves **overnight to ~3 days** and lessens with continued use. Treating at night is to hide this.

**Watch closely / yellow flags:**
- **Temporary skin darkening = post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH):** the healing inflammation makes melanocytes over-produce melanin, leaving treated areas *darker* than surrounding skin; it usually fades over weeks–months as skin turns over. (Distinct from the normal **"small brown dots"** = MTZ crusts/MENDs that slough off in days.) It affected **27% (16/60)** in the trial, and **5 of 60 lasted >3 months** → IFU says discontinue. Risk is higher in **Fitzpatrick III–VI / melasma-prone** skin, and **density drives PIH more than energy** ([Chan et al. 2010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21246574/); [08 §3c](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)) — so **stay low-level + daily SPF**. **This is the key risk for your pigment goal** — the device can darken before it ever helps.

**STOP and let it fully heal (or see a doctor) if:**
- Skin darkening **persists >3 months** → IFU says **discontinue use.**
- **Blistering, scabbing, crusting, raised/painful areas, or bleeding** — possible over-treatment or a more serious reaction.
- Skin still **red/swollen from a prior session** — wait until it's gone before treating again (don't stack sessions <24 h).

**Never use if:** under 18 · pregnant (untested) · on infected/cut/burned skin · over tattoos/permanent makeup · recovering from a peel/resurfacing/injectables/fillers · on body areas (only the face was tested).

## 8. Care, troubleshooting & the manuals

**Care:** wipe the **laser tip with a lint-free cloth** if it looks dirty (debris blocks the beam and breaks contact-sensing); store dry, away from water; keep on the cradle between uses so it's always charged.

**Quick troubleshooting (✅ IFU):**
| Symptom | Likely cause → fix |
|---|---|
| Won't power on | Locked → hold power until 4 beeps to unlock |
| Won't power off | You released too fast → hold power 1–2 s, release |
| Purple contact light won't stay on | Not flat / not moving / poor contact, **or dirty tip** → glide slow & flat; clean tip; recharge |
| Treatment sensation suddenly changed | Check level setting; clean tip |
| Fault indicator (**!**) lit | Power off then on; if it stays, contact Tria Customer Care |
| Won't charge in cradle | Reseat firmly; check cable both ends; try another outlet (battery light should flash) |

## 9. How this fits your bigger plan

- **Keep it for the collagen/texture/fine-line half of your goal.** It's the most transparently FDA-cleared home NAFL (brand-held 510(k) vs MimiSilk's manufacturer-held K182498) and a legitimate **maintenance/cumulative** tool — just not a Clear+Brilliant replacement ([08 §8](08_clear_brilliant_quantified_gap.md)).
- **Don't rely on it for pigment.** Wrong wavelength + explicitly disclaimed + PIH risk. For pigment, the evidence still favors **topicals + SPF**, and if you want a *device*, a verified **1927 nm** unit (clinic Perméa / a confirmed home Dora) — see [07 (parent)](../01_ipl_hair_removal/07_alternatives_and_strategy.md) and [09](09_1927nm_vs_1450nm_headtohead.md).
- **Biggest lever you control:** **consistency** (5 nights/wk, full 12-week course) and **ramping the level** (= density). That's how a low-density home device claws back ground over months ([11](11_subscriber_qa_lowdensity_rejuvenation.md)).
