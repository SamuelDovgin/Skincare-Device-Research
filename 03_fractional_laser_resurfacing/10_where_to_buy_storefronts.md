## 6. How to buy without getting burned

1. **Prefer brand-official or "authorized agent" with a warranty + return policy.** For YDUNVIE that's China-direct (Tmall flagship) or **myernk** (claims authorized, 12-mo warranty); for branded devices, the brand site or its official Amazon store.
2. **Watch for the dropship tell:** GlamGlobal, Sherue, and others share the **identical product slug** `fractional-laser-beauty-machine-dermabrasion-machine-...` — that's a re-listed dropship product, not an authorized stock holder. Same goes for OYAY.
3. **Demand the K-number + rating label** before paying premium for any "FDA-cleared" claim (MimiSilk/OYAY don't publish one; likely lean on Jiangsu Unimed K182498).
4. **Confirm wavelength on the label** — critical for the **1927nm Dora** (recurring 1450/1927 copy error).
5. **Price anchors:** China-direct A9 ≈ ¥3,000–3,400 (≈$417–$472). If a Western store charges $999 (OYAY) for the same hardware, you're paying ~2× for support/markup — fine if you want returns, bad if you assumed it was "better."
6. **Used PaloVia/RéAura:** buy only with photos of a working unit + (for RéAura) the gel, since these are consumables-dependent.
